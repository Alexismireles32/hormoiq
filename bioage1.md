## CORTISOL & BIOLOGICAL AGING

### Research Findings

**Baltimore Longitudinal Study (1,814 participants, 6.6-year follow-up):**
- Cortisol follows U-shaped pattern across lifespan
- Ages 20-30: Cortisol **decreases**
- Ages 40-50: Relatively **stable**
- Ages 60+: **Increases** significantly (20-50% higher than young adults)

**Key Numbers:**
- Mean cortisol increases by **10 nmol/L per decade** after age 50
- Older adults show **elevated nighttime cortisol** (disrupted circadian rhythm)
- Gender difference: Women have slightly **lower morning peaks** than men until menopause

### Normal Ranges by Age

**Morning Cortisol (8 AM, saliva):**
| Age Group | Optimal Range (ng/mL) | Population Mean |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------------|
| 20-30 | 8-20 | 14 |
| 31-40 | 8-18 | 13 |
| 41-50 | 8-16 | 12 |
| 51-60 | 9-19 | 14 |
| 61-70 | 10-22 | 16 |
| 71+ | 11-25 | 18 |

**Gender Adjustments:**
- Women (premenopausal): -1 to -2 ng/mL from male ranges
- Women (postmenopausal): Similar to male ranges
- Women on oral contraceptives: +2 to +3 ng/mL (CBG increase)

### BioAge Impact

**Leiden Longevity Study Finding:**
- High cortisol = Higher perceived age
- Offspring of long-lived families show **weaker cortisol-aging association** (enhanced stress resistance)

**Cortisol/DHEAS Ratio Study (2024):**
- Cortisol/DHEAS ratio is **strongest predictor** of epigenetic age acceleration
- Better than cortisol or DHEAS alone

### Algorithm Component: CORTISOL

```

- If in optimal range for age: 0 years

- If 80%+ tests in optimal range over 4+ weeks: -1 year
- If <40% tests in optimal range: +1 year

Final Cortisol Contribution: Sum of Steps 1-3
```

## DHEA/DHEAS & BIOLOGICAL AGING

### Research Findings

**MOST DRAMATIC DECLINE OF ALL HORMONES:**
- DHEA peaks at ages **20-25**
- By age 70: **10-20% of peak levels** (80-90% decline!)
- Decline rate: **2-3% per year** starting age 30
- Called "adrenopause" - maintenance of cortisol with collapse of DHEA

**DHEAge Study (280 participants, 1-year follow-up):**
- 50 mg/day DHEA supplementation **normalized aging effects**
- Improved skin, bone density (women), muscle (men)
- No harmful accumulation
